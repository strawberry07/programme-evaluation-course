Course Outline

Course Description

This course is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills in conducting programme evaluation for human service programmes. The course will cover programme evaluation as an integral part of professional practice, introduce concepts and methods from conceptualization to outcome stages, and discuss special topics such as cost-effectiveness analysis, cost-benefit analysis, performance indicators, statistical process control, benchmarking, reengineering, single-subject designs, and group designs.

Intended Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Evaluate the major paradigms and models of programme evaluation;
  2. Possess skills in finding and reviewing relevant literature in conducting programme evaluation;
  3. Digest and scrutinize academic and professional programme evaluation reports;
  4. Apply programme evaluation concepts and tools in their professional and daily work practices;
  5. Design and conduct independent programme evaluations for social programs that they are familiar with; and
  6. Generate findings based on quantitative and qualitative evaluation methods.

Assessment Details

Mid-Term Exam (20%) - 9/2/2026, YIA403

Students must sit for a mid-term examination. The examination questions may be set in forms of True/False statements, multiple choice questions, fill-in-blank questions, and short-answered questions. Students are encouraged to read not only the lecture notes but also recommended materials to deepen understandings of the course content.

Group Programme Evaluation Presentation (30%) - Submission deadline: 10/4/2026, 12pm

Students will form a group of six to complete a 15-minute oral presentation. Your group will present to potential funders a programme evaluation plan for an imaginary one-year social service programme. The presentation should include but not limited to the following elements, and the presentation will be evaluated according to feasibility, logic reasoning, evidence-based arguments and clear delivery. The texts in the presentation slides should be in English. To avoid free-riding, a peer-review questionnaire will be sent to each student.

Programme planning/Needs assessment:

  • The change you plan to achieve: what would change after your programme? How large do you expect the changes will be? Why achieving this change is important?
  • What will the programme look like? (Logic model)
  • Why do you think the logic model would work as you planned? (Theory of Change)

Outcome evaluation:

  • Through what methods will you find out whether your outcome is achieved or not?
  • Discuss both scenarios: outcome achieved vs. outcome not achieved. If your outcome is achieved as planned, what's the next step? If your outcome is not achieved as planned, what could be possible reasons? What insights do you gain from this "failure"?

Process evaluation:

  • What facilitators and barriers would there be for your implementation? What will you do to make sure the implementation of the programmes as planned and smoothly? What is your contingency plan if anything goes wrong to compromise the implementation of your programme?

Cost-effectiveness evaluation:

  • How much money are you asking for? Justify your budget with realistic assumptions and explain why your allocation is cost-effective compared to similar programmes.

Final Exam (50%) - 20/4/2026, 7-9pm, YIA403

Students must sit for an examination in the final lecture. The examination questions may be set in forms of True/False statements, multiple choice questions, short-answered questions, or long-answered questions. Frequently Asked Questions

What are the language requirements for written tasks and oral presentations?

+

All written tasks (e.g., written texts in group presentation slides, mid-term exam and final exam) should be in English. However, when students orally present their group project, they can use whatever language they are comfortable using. If they are going to speak a non-English language, they should make sure the written content is comprehensive enough and self-explanatory to all students.

What content will be covered in the mid-term and final exams?

+

The mid-term and final exam will be solely from lectures. However, reading materials and texts will be helpful for students to understand the lecture content more comprehensively.
